Bolt shear margin - NASA-TM-2012-217454 Section 3.1

Head type affects installation torque but not shear strength. For shear calculations, only diameter and grade matter.

The material strength rating. Higher number = stronger bolt. ISO 8.8 is standard construction, 10.9 and 12.9 are high-strength.
The shank diameter in mm, not the thread outer diameter. For M8 bolt, enter 8.
The force trying to slide the bolt sideways, perpendicular to its axis. NOT the tightening force.
Single shear = bolt cuts in one place, like a hinge pin. Double shear = bolt supported both sides, cuts in two places. Double shear is roughly twice as strong.
A safety multiplier on the applied load. Use 1.0 for well-defined loads, 1.15 for typical aerospace joints, 1.5 for uncertain load paths.

Minimum recommended: 1.0 × bolt diameter for steel, 1.5 × diameter for aluminium. Used in future thread shear calculations.

Preload affects fatigue life and joint separation. Preload analysis coming in v1.1.
